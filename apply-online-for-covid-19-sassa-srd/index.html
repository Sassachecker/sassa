Apply Online For Covid-19 Sassa SRD R350: Excellent Guide

  • Post published:March 30, 2023

The South African government introduces the SASSA Social Relief of Distress (SRD) grant fund in response to the economic impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic. The SRD grant is a temporary program aimed at helping the citizens of South Africa who were in dire need of financial support.

SRD grant is available for unemployed people, unable to work due to the pandemic, and do not receive any other form of a social grant or unemployment insurance. The grant has been a lifeline for many vulnerable individuals and families during these challenging times.

The SRD grant fund is critical in ensuring that the most vulnerable members of South African society are supported during these unprecedented times. Here we will tell you how to apply online for covid-19 Sassa SRD R350.

How to apply online for covid-19 Sassa sRD r350?

To apply online for the COVID-19 SASSA SRD R350 grant, please follow these steps:

  1. Visit the SASSA their official website.
  2. Then enter your phone number and click the “send sms” button.
  3. Then you will receive an “OTP” on your phone number.
  4. Next, enter the OTP and verify your PIN code.
  5. Read and accept the terms and conditions.
  6. Confirm your eligibility for the grant by answering a series of questions.
  7. Submit your application.

Please remember that processing times may be delayed due to the high volume of applications. To avoid delays or rejection of your application, you must provide accurate information and meet the eligibility criteria.

Eligibility Criteria for SASSA srd grant 

The eligibility criteria for the SASSA SRD grant are as follows:

  1. Applicants must be South African citizens, permanent residents, or refugees registered on the Home Affairs system to apply for the Sassa grant.
  2. Applicants must be above the age of 18.
  3. Applicants must not be registered for the UIF COVID-19 TERS benefit.
  4. Applicants must not be receiving any other government-funded financial assistance.
  5. Applicants must have a valid South African ID or asylum seeker permit.
  6. Applicants must provide valid contact details, including a cellphone number and/or an email address.
  7. Applicants must be eligible for the financial assistance criteria due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

How Sassa online portal has become an advantage for South African citizens?

Applying online for SASSA  grants has become a significant advantage for South African citizens in many ways. Here are some of the key benefits of applying for SASSA grants online:

Convenience

Online application is convenient for those who cannot visit a SASSA office in person. This may be due to distance, transportation issues, or mobility challenges. With an online application, applicants can complete the process from the comfort of their own homes or other convenient locations.

Time-saving

Applying online for SASSA grants can save time. Online applications can be completed at any time, from anywhere, making it a time-saving option for applicants who may have busy schedules or limited free time.

Faster Processing

Online applications can be processed more quickly than paper applications. Once the application is submitted, it is immediately available for review and processing by SASSA officials. This means that applicants may receive a response or payment more quickly.

Reduced Errors

Online applications are less prone to errors than paper applications. This is because the online application system will automatically flag incomplete or incorrect information and prompt the applicant to correct it. This helps reduce errors and ensures the application is processed more quickly.

Transparency

The online application process provides greater transparency in the application process. Applicants can track the progress of their application and receive real-time updates on the status of their application. This transparency helps to build trust between the applicant and SASSA officials.

Reduced Fraud

Online applications aid in the reduction of application fraud. The technology can authenticate the application and detect any fraudulent activities. This helps to guarantee that only those who are eligible receive SASSA funds.

Accessibility

Applying online makes the process more accessible to people with disabilities or special needs. For example, the online application can be adapted to meet the needs of applicants with visual or auditory impairments, making the process more accessible to everyone.

What to do if your Sassa srd r350 application is rejected?

If your SASSA application is rejected, there are several steps you can take to try and rectify the situation. Here are some of the things you can do:

  1. The first step is to find out why your application was rejected. This information will help you understand what went wrong and how to fix the problem. Then, you can contact the SASSA office or the call center for more information about the rejection.
  2. You can appeal the decision if you believe your application was rejected unfairly. You will need to provide additional information to support your appeal. You can visit the SASSA website or office for appealing.
  3. If your application was rejected due to errors or missing information, you can correct these issues and reapply. Ensure you provide all the necessary information and documentation to support your application.
  4. If you struggle to understand why your application was rejected or how to fix the problem, you can seek assistance from a social worker or community worker.

Why Covid-19 social distress grants extended?

Due to ongoing inflation and unemployment, the COVID-19 social distress grant was extended to 31 March 2024. The reduced incomes, and increased living expenses, have left many vulnerable individuals and families struggling.

The COVID-19 social distress grants were extended in response to requests from various stakeholders, including civil society organizations and political parties, who argued that the grant’s initial three-month period was insufficient to provide adequate relief to those in need. 

In the budget speech, Finance Minister Enoch Godongwana acknowledged the ongoing economic impact of the pandemic and the need for continued financial assistance to the most vulnerable.

The minister budgeted up to R36.1 billion in his Budget Speech for 2023/2024 to keep funding the R350 grant, launched in 2020, to assist poor households in the aftermath of the pandemic.
