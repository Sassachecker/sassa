Budget Speech Anti Poor Over SASSA Grant Allocations

  • Post published:March 30, 2023

The budget speech by the finance minister is an important event in the country’s political calendar. It outlines the government’s spending and revenue generation plans over the coming fiscal year and is closely watched by analysts, investors, and citizens alike. Know more about Budget Speech Anti Poor Over SASSA Grant Allocations.

The black sash has labeled the budget speech anti-poor over Sassa regulations for allocating funds. The human rights organization hoped that with the ongoing inflation rate, the funds would be increased according to the inflation, but after the speech, they were disappointed.

One of the most contentious issues surrounding the budget speech has been allocating funds for the SASSA, which is responsible for administering grants to vulnerable and impoverished citizens. The grants include old-age pensions, disability, child support, and others.

Why is budget speech labeled as “anti-poor” over Sassa grant allocations?

Critics have been done on the allocated budget, and black sash has accused the finance minister of being anti-poor, arguing that the allocation of funds for SASSA grants is inadequate and does not adequately address the needs of the country’s poorest citizens. 

This has led to protests and demonstrations, particularly by civil society organizations representing the interests of the poor and marginalized. In the budget speech, the finance minister announced the increased funding for temporary and permanent grants.

However, critics argue that this increase is not enough to address the scale of poverty and inequality in the country and that more needs to be done to support vulnerable citizens. In particular, there has been raising concern among the citizen of South Africa about over-allocating funds, which provide a lifeline to families struggling to make ends meet. 

Some analysts have argued that the government should consider increasing the grant amount according to the inflation rate and expanding eligibility criteria to ensure that more families can access this vital form of assistance.

Sassa grant allocations in Budget 2023

In recent years, the (SASSA has faced criticism over the adequacy of its grant allocations for vulnerable and impoverished citizens. However, the agency announced an increase in grant allocations for the year.

The Minister of Social Development, Enoch Godongwana, announced the increase in grant allocations in March 2023. The increase in funds is implemented to boost the income of grant recipients.

  • The old age pension was increased from R1985 to R2085 per month.
  • While the disability grant is increased from R1900 to R2085 per month. 
  • Old Age grant for people whose age is above 75 and War Veterans grant increased from R2,005 to R2,105
  • The child support grant is increased from R480 to R505 per month
  • The grant for foster care increased from R1070 to R1,125 per month. 
  • The care dependency grant is also increased by R1985 to R2085 per month.

The increase in grant allocations was welcomed by many South Africans, who recognized the importance of social welfare programs in supporting vulnerable citizens. However, like the temporary relief programs, the social distress grant allocated to SASSA will not increase R350.

Due to no increment in the SRD grant R350 grant has raised many questions by the human rights organization that R350 is enough for a person to survive for a month. The critics and debates continue with the “anti-poor “budget to address poverty and inequality.

FAQs About Budget Speech Anti Poor Over SASSA Grant

SASSA grants are social welfare grants provided by the South African government to help support the poorest and most vulnerable members of society, including the elderly, disabled, and children.

SASSA provides temporary and permanent social grants to eligible individuals in South Africa. Temporary grants are intended to provide short-term assistance to individuals experiencing financial hardship.

In contrast, permanent grants are meant to provide ongoing support to those unable to support themselves due to age, disability, or other factors.

To qualify for a SASSA grant, individuals must meet certain eligibility criteria, including income and asset tests. Applications are then processed by SASSA, which assesses whether the applicant meets the criteria for the specific grant.

The government can work to ensure that its fiscal policies are truly inclusive and supportive of all members of society, including the poorest and most vulnerable. This may involve increasing funding for social welfare programs like SASSA grants and improving transparency and accountability in the allocation process.
