How to Cancel SASSA Relief Grant SRD 350

  • Post published:April 16, 2024

Imagine a tap, steadily dripping funds into your bank account each month – this is the SASSA Relief Grant SRD 350. But what if your financial condition is improved and want to turn off that tap? Whether you’ve found a job or no longer meet the criteria, you may need to cancel this grant.

In this guide of Sassa status check, we’ll peel back the layers of red tape, showing you the step-by-step procedure of How to Cancel SASSA Relief Grant SRD 350.

Reasons to Cancel SASSA Relief Grant SRD 350

You’re in need to cancel the SRD 350 grant. Maybe you’ve found employment and no longer require financial assistance, or perhaps Sassa status check showed that grant was accidentally applied for or received.

The most common reason for cancellation is securing a job. The grant’s purpose is to provide temporary financial relief to those unemployed due to the Covid-19 pandemic. If you’re gainfully employed.

Another reason could be a change in your financial situation. Maybe you’ve come into an inheritance or received a significant amount of money. In such cases, it’s best to cancel the grant because in these situations canceling this grant is essential otherwise you may face a lawsuit. Falsely getting this payment may create a hurdle for you.

Ways to Cancel Your Sassa Relief Grant SRD 350

There are three official ways to cancel your SRD Grant through SASSA:

1. Online via the SASSA SRD Grant Website:

  1. Visit the SASSA SRD Grant website: https://srd.sassa.gov.za/said
  2. Click on the “Manage My Account” button.
  3. Enter your South African ID number and registered mobile phone number.
  4. Click on “Log in.”
  5. Once logged in, navigate to the “Manage Grant” section.
  6. Click on the “Cancel Grant” option.
  7. Review the information provided and confirm your cancellation request.

You will receive an SMS notification confirming your cancellation.

2. Emailing SASSA

  1. Draft an email
  2. In the subject line, clearly state “Request to Cancel SRD Grant.”
  3. In the email body, include the following information:
    • The reason for canceling your grant (optional, but helpful).
    • Your registered mobile phone number.
    • Your full name and 13-digit South African ID number.
  4. Proofread your email and send it to GrantsEnquiries@sassa.gov.za.
  5. You will receive a confirmation email or SMS within 5-7 business days.

3. WhatsApp (Currently Unavailable):

SASSA previously offered grant cancellation via WhatsApp, but this service is currently unavailable. They recommend using the website or email options.

Frequently Asked Questions

What Is the Eligibility Criteria for Applying to the SASSA Relief Grant SRD 350?

You’re eligible if you’re a South African citizen, over 18, unemployed, not receiving any income or social grant, and not residing in a government-funded institution. It’s crucial to meet these conditions.

How Long Does It Take for the Cancellation of SASSA Relief Grant SRD 350 to Be Processed?

You’re wondering about the cancellation processing time for the SASSA Relief Grant SRD 350. It’s not set in stone, but typically, you can expect it to take around 30 days for your cancellation request to be processed.

Can I Reapply for the SASSA Relief Grant SRD 350 After Cancellation?

Yes, you can reapply for the SASSA relief grant SRD 350 after cancellation. However, it’s essential to know the reapplication process may require fresh eligibility checks and could take some time to get approved.

Are There Any Penalties or Repercussions for Cancelling the SRD 350 Grant?

No, there aren’t any penalties or repercussions for canceling the SRD 350 grant. You’re free to cancel it anytime you wish, without worrying about any negative impact on your future applications or financial standing.

What Are Alternative Financial Support Options After Cancelling the SASSA Relief Grant SRD 350?

After canceling your SASSA relief grant, you’ve multiple alternatives for financial support. You could apply for unemployment benefits, seek employment, or explore other government aid programs. It’s all about finding what fits your situation best.

Conclusion

Wrapping up, you’ve learned how to cancel your SASSA SRD 350 grant and the reasons you might need to. Don’t forget that canceling your grant is irreversible for the current grant period. If you wish to reapply in the future, you will need to submit a new application.
