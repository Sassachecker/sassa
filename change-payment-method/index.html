How to Change Payment Method for SRD R350 Grant

  • Post published:April 16, 2024

Ever wondered if you can change your payment method for your SRD R350 Grant? Well, you can, and it’s easier now. Sassa status check has officially tweeted that individuals can now change their payment methods for the Sassa SRD R350 grant at their convenience. Before their official tweet and update, the individuals weren’t allowed to update the new payment methods but it is easier now.

In this guide, you’ll learn How to Change the Payment Method for the SRD R350 Grant and discover the various withdrawal options available to you.

Change Method For SRD R350 Grant

To ensure smooth transactions when receiving your SRD R350 Grant, you’ll need to keep a few key tips in mind. First of all, check below the payment methods available for you to collect your grant.

Available Withdrawal Options

At the time of receiving your SRD R350 Grant, you’re presented with several withdrawal options to choose from. Understanding these options is crucial in ensuring that you can access your funds in the most convenient way possible.

  1. The first option is a direct bank transfer. This approach enables the transfer of funds directly into the bank account.
  2. You’ll need to provide your banking details, including the account number and branch code.
  3. Another method is through a money transfer service such as Post Office or Cash Send. However, you’ll need to provide a valid ID and the transaction number that’s sent to your mobile phone.
  4. Thirdly, Shoprite Group Stores if available. This option is available in select Shoprite, Checkers, and Usave stores throughout South Africa. However, the Shoprite Group Store is not offered at all locations, so you need to ensure this before proceeding. Unlike other withdrawal options, there are no additional fees for collecting your grant at Shoprite stores.

In addition to these payment methods, some sources also stated, that since 2023, SASSA discontinued the option of using bank mobile money transfer (cash send option) due to security concerns.

Another method is the virtual cards, Although not officially offered by SASSA, some private companies provide virtual cards linked to your grant, but these typically involve fees and additional steps.

Step-by-Step Payment Method Change

So, how do you go about changing your payment method for the SRD R350 Grant if you’ve already chosen one? It’s quite straightforward.

  1. First, navigate to the SASSA SRD website srd.sassa.gov.za
  2. Once there, look for the ‘SRD Grant’ section. Click on it and you’ll be redirected to a page where you’ll find the ‘Change Payment Method’ or “How do I change my banking details”.
  3. Enter your South African ID number and cell no:
  4. Sassa will send an SMS having a link in it to your mobile for further proceeding steps.
  5. Also, you’ll get the OTP.
  6. Open the link in the SMS and follow the instructions provided.
  7. Enter the new bank account details which you want to use this includes bank name, branch code, and account number.
  8. Review your information for accuracy and confirm the changes.
  9. You will soon get a confirmation message if the update is successful.

How to Withdraw SASSA Grant Money From a Bank Account?

There are two options available for the grant money to get. One is the ATM and the second is from the bank branch.

ATM:

  1. The most accessible option.
  2. Locate any ATM displaying the SASSA or Mastercard logo.
  3. Insert the card of SASSA if you have
  4. Enter your PIN
  5. Select “Cash Withdrawal.”

Bank Branch:

  1. Visit your bank branch with your SASSA card and ID.
  2. A teller can assist you with withdrawing your grant.

How to Withdraw SASSA Grant Money From Post Office or Cash Send?

CashSend Services:

  1. Receive SMS Notification: Once your grant is ready, you’ll receive an SMS with a One-Time Pin (OTP).
  2. Locate a Participating Outlet: Check the SASSA website for a list of participating retail outlets, like Pick n Pay or Boxer Superstores (availability may vary by region).
  3. Present ID and OTP: At the chosen outlet, provide your ID and the OTP received via SMS to the cashier.
  4. Collect Cash: You’ll receive your grant amount in cash.

Post Office Cash:

  1. Locate a Participating Post Office: Not all post offices offer this service, so check the SASSA website for participating locations.
  2. Present ID and Grant Notification: Show your ID and any grant payment notification you may have received to the postal worker.
  3. Collect Cash: You’ll receive your grant amount in cash directly at the post office.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are There Any Penalties or Fees for Changing the Payment Method for the SRD R350 Grant?

No, there aren’t any penalties or fees for changing your payment method. You’re free to switch how you receive your SRD R350 grant without any additional costs.

Can You Split the SRD R350 Grant Payment Between Different Payment Methods?

No, you can’t split the SRD R350 grant payment between different methods. You’ll need to select one payment method, whether it’s a direct deposit into your bank account or through a mobile money transfer.

Can I Change the Payment Method to Someone Else’s Bank Account or Mobile Wallet?

Yes, you can change the payment method for your SRD R350 grant. However, it’s crucial to ensure the new account or mobile wallet is in your name. They don’t allow payments to third-party accounts.

What Should I Do if I Encounter an Error or Issue While Changing the Payment Method for My SRD R350 Grant?

If you’re facing a hurdle in changing your payment method, don’t panic. Contact the SRD R350 Grant’s customer service immediately. They’ll guide you through any issues you’re experiencing.

Conclusion

So, there you have it now how to change the payment method for the SRD R350 grant! Changing your payment method for the SRD R350 grant is as simple as following the steps provided. With various withdrawal options available, you’re not stuck with one choice.

Remember, a smooth transaction comes from ensuring your details are up-to-date and correct. Don’t forget to stay on top of any changes to keep receiving your grant without a hitch.
