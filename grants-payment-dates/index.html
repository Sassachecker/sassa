SASSA Grants Payment Dates

  • Post published:April 16, 2024

The South African Social Security Agency SASSA is owned by the government and provides grants to over 5 million South Africans on demand by the Department of Social Development. SASSA has provided various channels to check the SASSA grant payment dates of all types. Our official website https://sassachecker.co.za/ provides all the dates for checking grants dates in one place. You can also find the SASSA Status Check for SRD 350 on our website.

As a beneficiary of South Africa’s Social Security Agency (SASSA) grants, you’ll find it imperative to stay updated on specific payment dates for different types of grants. Whether you’re receiving the Child Support Grant, the Older Person’s Grant, or the Disability Grant, knowing the scheduled payment dates can help you manage your funds better.

It is crucial to keep yourself up to date on all the grants and the Status of your SRD payments. You don’t need to wander for future payment dates up to the extended date of 7 March 2024. you might be wondering, who’s eligible for these grants? This includes older persons, people with disabilities, war veterans, and caregivers. Not to forget, children under the age of 18 also fall under the umbrella.

Not only these but some other terms and conditions are used to provide these SRD 350 grants for example unemployed citizens between 18 to 60 age. They’re monthly payments aimed at providing consistent support.

What is the SASSA Social Relief of Distress R350 Grant?

Building on the notion of financial aid, you might’ve heard of the SASSA Social Relief of Distress R350 Grant and wondered what it’s all about.

The South African Social Security Agency (SASSA) SRD350 was initially created for people who don’t have any income to provide them temporary relief from COVID-19. It’s particularly targeted at those who are most vulnerable: the unemployed, individuals unable to work due to illness, and those who can’t secure assistance from other social grants.

The R350 grant doesn’t require you to pay it back. It’s a form of aid, not a loan. It’s a temporary relief measure intended to help you.

To access this grant, a South African citizen must pass the criteria for the grant. You must have a valid SA ID card number, must be unemployed, and not receive any other grant from other social networks. You must also be above 18 and below 60 years old.

SASSA Grant Payment Schedule

The South African Social Security Agency (SASSA) typically disburses funds during the first week of each month. However, checking your registered mobile number for SMS updates from SASSA is best. You must be checking the status of your payments on the official website. https://sassachecker.co.za/ We have mentioned all the payment dates below for the future too.

All Types of SASSA Grants Payment Dates

SASSA grants are typically disbursed in a particular sequence to avoid congestion at payment points and ensure smooth transactions.

First, payments for Older Persons’ grants start on the 3rd of each month. If the third day of the month coincides with a weekend or a public holiday, the disbursement of payments will take place on the closest preceding business day.

Next, disability grants are paid from the 4th of each month. Again, if the 4th is a weekend or public holiday, the payments are made on the nearest workday before the 4th.

Lastly, all other grants, including the Child Support Grant are paid from the 5th of each month. If the 5th falls on a weekend or public holiday, the payouts happen on the nearest working day before the 5th.

How to Check SASSA R350 Grant Status Online?

First, visit the official SASSA SRD (Social Relief of Distress) website https://sassachecker.co.za/. You’ll have an option to ‘Check your Status’, select that. 

You need to input your ID number and the mobile phone number you provided to the SASSA while registering for the grant. After filling in, hit the ‘Submit’ button. In a few moments, your grant status will appear on the screen.
