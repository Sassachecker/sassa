How to Apply for R350 Grant Using GovChat

  • Post published:April 16, 2024

Navigating the R350 Grant application process with GovChat is as simple as walking in a park and is more convenient. Gone are the days when citizens needed to rush to SASSA’s office to fill out the application form. With the innovations, now the eligible individuals can apply using the GovChat.

This handy guide will help you understand how to apply for R350 grant using GovChat, how to do Sassa status check via efficient government communication service, to apply for the R350 grant.

You’ll learn how to register for a GovChat account, initiate the grant application, and track your application status.

About GovChat App

Before you start your application process, you must understand what GovChat is and how it works.

  • GovChat, simply put, is an online platform that connects you directly with your government.
  • You can use GovChat to access a variety of government services, including applying for grants. It’s a valuable tool if you’re seeking the R350 grant.

Once you’ve registered, you can start using GovChat’s services. You can search for services, send requests, and report issues. You’ll be also able to receive updates on the status of your requests. It’s all in real time.

Registering for GovChat Account

  1. First, find and download the GovChat app available at Google Play Store or visit their website (www.govchat.org.za)
  2. After the installation is complete, launch the application and select the ‘Register’ option.
  3. You will be prompted to input fundamental details like your full name, email address, and phone number.
  4. Following this, create a secure password to enhance the security of your account.
  5. Subsequently, you’ll be asked to review and agree to the terms and conditions.
  6. After agreeing to the terms, complete the process by selecting the ‘Register’ button. 
  7. Following this, you’ll receive a confirmation email or SMS containing a verification code.
  8. Input this code in the specified field to confirm and proceed. Once the verification is successful, your GovChat account is set up, and you’re ready to use the application.

Navigating the GovChat Platform

  • The main dashboard presents you with several options. These include ‘Services’, ‘Chat’, ‘Notifications’, and ‘Profile’.
  • To apply for the R350 grant, head to the ‘Services’ section. You’ll get a list of services offered by the government. Look for the ‘Social Relief of Distress Grant’. Proceed with clicking on this option to start the application process.
  • The ‘Chat’ option allows you to communicate directly with government representatives. If you’re confused or need help.
  • The ‘Notifications’ tab keeps you updated with any new developments or changes in your application status. Remember to check it frequently.

Last but not least, you’ll find your ‘Profile’ where you can manage your personal information, and account settings, and access your application history.

How to Apply for R350 Grant Using GovChat

  1. So you’ve registered with the GovChat, you’ll need to click on the ‘Services’ tab located at the top of the GovChat website.
  2. Once there, you’ll find an array of services listed. Scroll down until you find the ‘Social Relief of Distress (SRD) Grant’ and click on it.
  3. After that, you’ll see the option for ‘Application for Social Relief of Distress’.
  4. Click on ‘Apply for SRD’ and you’ll be redirected to another section for filling out the form for application. Here, you’ll need to fill in the details required which means about yourself name and ID number, etc.
  5. Next, you’ll be asked to provide your banking details.
  6. This is where your grant money will be deposited if your application is approved. 
  7. After this step, simply accept the terms and conditions of the GovChat.

Tracking Your Application Status

Following your application submission, you must keep track of your R350 grant application status on GovChat.

  • Log into your GovChat account > ‘My Applications’ section. Here, you’ll see all your active applications, including the status of your SRD grant application.
  • The status of your application may vary. It could be ‘Pending’, signaling that your application is under review. If it’s ‘Approved’, congratulations! You’ve been granted the R350 grant. ‘Declined’ means your application wasn’t successful.
  • You can always reapply. ‘Paid’ means your grant has been disbursed, and ‘Awaiting Payment’ means your payment is being processed.

If you face any problems or require help, GovChat provides a ‘Help’ section, or you can reach out to their support team

Final Thoughts

In conclusion, applying for the R350 grant through GovChat can be a seamless process if you’re diligent and proactive. Not only you can apply for the grant but also stay informed about your application’s progress.

The final decision lies with the South African Social Security Agency (SASSA). They’re responsible for reviewing your application and determining your eligibility. Thus, ensure all the information about yourself provided is accurate.
