Ways to Check SASSA Status

Checking your SASSA status can be done through a couple of straightforward methods.

  1. First, you can check online via the SASSA SRD website https://sassachecker.co.za/.
  2. The second method is via USSD. It’s as simple as dialling 1347737# from your phone. Once you dial the USSD code enter your valid SA ID number and the Cell number you used to register. Your status will be sent to your phone via SMS. The USSD code connection is encrypted, ensuring your personal information remains secure during the process.
  3. You can also check your status via WhatsApp. Just send a message saying ‘Status’ to 082 046 8553. You’ll need to respond with your SA ID number and cell number when prompted.
  4. If you’ve downloaded the SASSA mobile app then you can also check the status of your grant.
  5. Lastly, you can check via email. Send an email to SRD@sassa.gov.za with ‘Status Check’ as the subject. Include your ID number and phone number in the email body. You’ll receive a reply with your status. Below we’ve also described the additional ways to check your status.

Eligibility Criteria For SASSA SRD R350

  1. South African citizen and permanent resident.
  2. A refugee registered on the Home Affairs system.
  3. You’re not eligible if you’re receiving any income, have a job, or get any kind of government assistance.
  4. You can’t apply if you’re getting unemployment insurance benefits or have an application pending at UIF.
  5. Students who receive NSFAS stipends aren’t eligible either.
  6. Your age should be more than 18 years and below 60.
  7. Your annual income must be below the SASSA threshold. As of October 2023, this threshold is R587 500 for a family of 4. R79 876 for individuals.

Necessary Documents

To apply for the SASSA SRD R350 grant, you’ll need to provide some essential documents and details.

  1. You’ll need your South African ID number or Department of Home Affairs permit number if you’re a refugee.
  2. You’ll also need your contact details: phone number and email address.
  3. To receive the grant, you must confirm you’re not receiving any other social benefits or unemployment insurance funds.
  4. Additionally, you’ll need to provide your bank information, including bank name, branch, and account number.
  5. If you don’t own a bank account, receiving your grant through a cash-send service is an option available.
  6. Lastly, you’ll need to provide proof of your residential address. This could be a recent utility bill or a signed lease agreement.

Remember, all these documents and details must be current and accurate.

All SASSA Grant Types

You’ll find there are seven different types of SASSA grants available to eligible South African citizens and residents.

1. The first is the Older Person’s Grant, often known as the old age pension. If you’re over 60 and unable to support yourself, you may qualify for it. The eligibility criteria are 60 years or older, annual income below the SASSA threshold (around R79 876 as of October 2023),

Grant amount: R1,910 per month (as of October 2023).

2. The Disability Grant: It is available for those who are permanently or temporarily unable to work due to a disability. Therefore, your annual income is below the SASSA threshold.

Grant amount: R1,910 per month

3. Care Dependency Grant: Support for individuals caring for a person with a disability who receives a disability grant or is permanently dependent on you.

Grant amount: R1,910 per month

4. Foster Child Grant: Financial assistance for individuals legally fostering a child under 18 years old.

Grant amount: R1,070 per month per child.

5. Child Support Grant: Support for primary caregivers of children under 18 years old. Combined household income below the SASSA threshold (around R587 500 for a family of 4 as of October 2023).

Grant amount: R460 per month per child.

6. War Veterans Grant: The War Veterans Grant in South Africa provides financial support and recognition to individuals who served in the South African military during declared wars or conflicts.

Having served in the South African military during a declared war or conflict, such as the Border War, World War II, or the South African Communist Party struggle also meets the requirements of disability.

Grant amount: Varies depending on individual circumstances and disability level.

7. Social Relief of Distress (SRD) Grant (R350): Temporary financial assistance for individuals experiencing severe hardship due to unemployment and lack of alternative income due to COVID-19.

Grant amount: R350 per month

SASSA Grant Results Meanings

Once you’ve determined which SASSA grant best suits your situation, it’s crucial to understand the different results statuses such as ‘Pending’ or ‘Declined’ that you might encounter during the application process. These statuses provide information on the progression of your application.

‘Pending‘ status indicates your application is not been accepted yet but the team is reviewing it. It’s not necessarily a bad sign, but rather means that SASSA is processing your application and you need to wait patiently. Generally, it takes some time for SASSA to verify your eligibility before changing the status.

‘Declined‘ status means your application wasn’t successful. This could be due to various reasons like insufficient documentation, ineligibility as per SASSA’s rules, or maybe an error in your application.

‘Approved‘ this status means Congratulations! Your application has been successfully accepted, and you can expect to begin receiving your grant payments shortly. The status report should include the payout amount, date, and reference ID.

What Happens if My SASSA SRD R350 Grant Application Is Declined?

If your application is declined, don’t panic. You’re allowed to appeal the decision within 90 days. Gather all necessary documents, submit your appeal, and patiently wait the review.

Can I Apply for the SASSA R350 Grant if I Am Currently Employed?

No, you can’t apply for the SASSA R350 grant if you’re currently employed. This grant is specifically designed to assist those unemployed with no other form of income during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Can the SASSA Grant Be Transferred to Someone Else on My Behalf?

No, you can’t transfer the SASSA grant to someone else on your behalf. The grant is strictly for the individual who applied and qualifies. This is to ensure the benefits reach the intended recipients.

Are There Any Penalties for Providing Incorrect Information on the SASSA Grant Application?

Yes, there are penalties for providing incorrect info on your SASSA grant application. You could face legal consequences like fines or imprisonment, and it can also lead to the cancellation of your grant.

What Is the Process for Appealing a Declined SASSA Grant Application?

To appeal a declined SASSA grant application, you’ll need to submit a written notice to SASSA within 90 days of receiving your rejection. Include your reasons for appealing and any supporting documents.
