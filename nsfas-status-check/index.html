NSFAS Status Check

  • Post published:April 16, 2024

So you’ve applied for the National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) in South Africa and you’re waiting to hear back. Each year, thousands of students apply for this scheme, making the NSFAS Status Check a critical part of the process.

This tool lets you track your application’s progress, ensuring you’re always in the loop. You can check if your application has been received, and approved, or if you need to submit any additional documents.

It’s a straightforward, efficient way to stay updated on your application and avoid any unnecessary stress or confusion. So, let’s start the guide on how you can use the NSFAS Status Check to your advantage.

Understanding the NSFAS Application Process

The first thing you need to know is that NSFAS takes time to review all applications.

During this time, NSFAS will be checking your application against the criteria they’ve set. These include your academic performance, financial need, and the course you have chosen. 

Once they’ve reviewed your application, you’ll receive your application outcome. It’ll be one of three: approved, pending, or rejected. If approved, you’ll receive details on your funding. If it’s pending, you might need to provide more information.

Ways to Check NSFAS Status And How to Check Status Using these ways

  • The online NSFAS Status Check Portal
  • The NSFAS Mobile App
  • NSFAS Website
  • USSD code

1. Online NSFAS Status Check Portal

Checking your NSFAS status online is a process that you can easily manage through the NSFAS Status Check Portal.

  1. You’ll need your NSFAS reference number and the cellphone number you provided.
  2. First, visit the NSFAS official site and click on ‘My NSFAS Account’.
  3. Enter your NSFAS username and password, then click ‘Sign In’.
  4. Once logged in, hit on ‘Track Funding Progress’.
  5. Here you’ll see the status of your NSFAS application.
  6. This portal not only helps you check your status but also provides updates on your NSFAS application.

2. NSFAS Mobile App

  1. There’s also an NSFAS mobile app that lets you check your application status conveniently. 
  2. You can download the app from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Once installed, log in using your username and password.
  3. There’s a dashboard that displays important information, including your application status.
  4. To check your status, tap on ‘My Application Status’. The app will display whether your application is successful, waiting for confirmation, or unsuccessful.

3. NSFAS Website

For a straightforward status update on your NSFAS application, the official NSFAS website is your go-to resource. You’ll need to visit the NSFAS website and log into your MyNSFAS account. 

Once logged in, click on the ‘Track Funding Progress’ tab. This will give you a real-time update of your application status.

If you’re stuck or encounter issues, there’s a ‘Contact Us’ page where you can submit a query or find the helpline number.

4. NSFAS status using a USSD code

  1. When you’re on the go, you can also check your NSFAS status using a USSD code. This is a quick and easy way to stay updated on your application status.
  2. All you need is to dial 120176# and follow the prompts.
  3. You’ll need your ID number handy, as this is required for verification.
  4. Wait for a few minutes, you’ll receive an SMS with your current NSFAS status.
  5. Remember, this method will consume a small amount of airtime or data, so make sure you’re prepared for that. Also, the USSD code service is available 24/7.

5. NSFAS Contact Centre

You can reach them via phone at 0860 067 327 or by email at info@nsfas.org.za.

Alternatively, you can use the NSFAS Connect feature on their website. You’ll need to log in to your myNSFAS account, click on the Connect tab, and then send a message.

6. Social Media

Besides contacting the NSFAS Contact Centre, you can also use social media platforms to check your NSFAS status.

  • NSFAS has official accounts on Facebook and Twitter. To check your status on Twitter, follow their account, @myNSFAS, then send them a direct message.
  • On Facebook, search for the ‘National Student Financial Aid Scheme’ page. Once you’ve liked and followed the page, you can send them a direct message.

Remember to provide your ID number and your application reference in your message.

7. Check myNSFAS Using WhatsApp

Now, let’s look at how you can check your NSFAS status using WhatsApp, a more convenient and immediate method for many.

  • First, you’ll need to add NSFAS as a contact. Save this number: +27 78 519 8006.
  • Once done, open WhatsApp and refresh your contacts. You’ll find NSFAS listed.

Start a chat by typing ‘Hi’. and ask them for  Application Status. They will ask the basic information like an ID card and application number number.

What is the Difference between the SASSA R350 Grant and NSFAS Status?

Let’s unpack these differences to help you understand which one could potentially benefit you the most.

SASSA SRD 350

In understanding the difference between the SASSA R350 grant and NSFAS status, you must be aware of their specific functions and benefits.

The SASSA R350 grant, also known as the Social Relief of Distress (SRD) grant, is a temporary provision of assistance for individuals in dire need. It’s generally offered to those unemployed and without any form of income or any other form of social grant.

The SASSA R350 grant is a monthly payment from the South African government to individuals who can’t support themselves or their dependents. 

NSFAS Status

Your NSFAS status refers to your application for financial aid to study at a public university or TVET college in South Africa.

NSFAS strives to guarantee that every deserving student, based on academic merit, has equitable opportunities for higher education, irrespective of their socio-economic status. This program is designed to provide loans, to students who are unable to pay their academic fees, accommodation, study materials, and living costs.

These funds are provided on a means-tested basis, taking into consideration the financial situation of the student and their family.

NSFAS Statuses and Their Meanings

Let’s get to grips with the different NSFAS statuses and what they mean.

  • From ‘Application Submitted’ to ‘Payment Phase’, each status represents a significant step in your NSFAS journey.
  • Understanding these can make the process less confusing and more manageable for you.

Application Submitted” Status

After you’ve submitted your NSFAS application, you’ll see the ‘Application Submitted’ status. This simply means that your application has been received and is under review.

You’ll not be able to interfere or influence the evaluation in any way. While waiting, ensure that all contact information in your application is correct.

Application Approved” Status

Once your NSFAS application moves beyond the ‘Application Submitted’ status, you might see the ‘Application Approved’ status, which signals that your funding application has been successful. This is a big sigh of relief, as it means the National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) has reviewed your application and deemed you eligible for financial aid.

However, it’s not the end of the process. Next, you’ll receive a formal letter detailing the amount and terms of your funding.

Funding Progress” Status

Next in the process, you’ll likely encounter the ‘Funding Progress’ status, which provides a significant update on your NSFAS journey. This status means NSFAS is finalizing your funding. It’s a positive sign, indicating that your application has moved from the initial approval stage to the final funding stage.

While awaiting your final approval, continue focusing on your academic goals. The ‘Funding Progress’ status is another step closer to realizing your dreams of further education.

Payment Phase” Status

Transitioning from the ‘Funding Progress’ status, you’ll now enter the ‘Payment Phase’ status, a critical stage in the NSFAS process denoting that your funding is about to be disbursed. This status means your application has been successful and the funds are ready to be released.

What to Do If I Get Rejected?

Firstly, understand why your application was rejected. There could be a variety of reasons, including incomplete information, missing documentation, or you may not meet the financial need criteria.

If it’s an issue of incomplete information or missing documents, ensure that you provide all the necessary details and paperwork when you reapply. If you didn’t meet the financial need criteria, you might need to provide more evidence of your financial situation.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Firstly, you’ll need to provide a valid ID to prove your citizenship.
  • Secondly, you must be applying to study at a recognized public university or TVET college. You won’t be eligible for NSFAS funding if you’re applying to a private institution.
  • Another requirement is that you must require financial assistance. You’ll need to prove that your household income is below a certain threshold. For the 2021 academic year, this threshold was set at R350,000 per annum for first-time entering students and R122,000 for returning students.
  • Lastly, you must be applying for an undergraduate degree, diploma, or certificate that’s your first higher education qualification. If you’ve already obtained a degree or diploma, you won’t be eligible for NSFAS funding.

Essential Documents

  • The most critical document is your proof of income. You’ll need to submit either your latest payslip if you’re employed or an affidavit if you’re unemployed.
  • Next, A certified copy of your South African ID or a birth certificate if you’re under 16. If you’re a dependent, your parent or guardian’s certified ID copy is also required.
  • Thirdly, make sure to have your academic records on hand. These include your latest school results or Grade 12 certificate and your university acceptance letter. These documents help the NSFAS ascertain your academic eligibility for funding.
  • Lastly, you’ll need to provide your contact details. This includes your cell phone number and email address to receive updates about your application status.

How to Submit Application

  • Firstly, navigate to the NSFAS website. Once you’re there, look for the ‘myNSFAS’ tab and click on it.
  • Once you’ve clicked on ‘myNSFAS’, you’ll be asked to create an account. It’s crucial to input correct and current personal information. Your email address and mobile number should be valid as they’ll be used for communication and updates regarding your application.
  • You can now proceed to the application section. Click on the ‘Apply’ tab. A form will appear, filled out with accurate and detailed information, and upload your documents.
  • When you’re done filling out the form and attaching your documents, review everything one last time.

Finally, click on the ‘Submit’ button. After this, you’ll get a confirmation message, either via email or SMS.

Ways To Receive NSFAS Payment

  • Firstly, the method is through a deposit into your bank account. NSFAS will require you to provide them with your banking details, including your bank name, account number, and branch code.
  • Secondly, you can receive your NSFAS funds through a cell phone banking system. Afterward, NSFAS will transfer the funds directly to your mobile banking account, which you can then access through your phone.
  • Another way to receive your NSFAS funds is through the National Student Financial Aid Scheme’s Wallet. Once NSFAS transfers your money to the NSFAS Wallet, you’ll receive a message notifying you of the funds. From there, you can withdraw the cash from any ATM or use it directly to pay for goods and services.

NSFAS can also pay your funds directly to the institution where you’re studying.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Apply for NSFAS if I Am Currently Employed?

Sure, you can apply even if you’re employed. However, your application’s success depends on your income. If it’s below the set threshold, you’re likely to qualify. You’ll need to provide proof of income though.

How Long Does It Take for NSFAS to Process My Application?

After you’ve submitted your application, it typically takes NSFAS about a month to process it. However, sometimes it can take longer.

Is There an Age Limit to Apply for NSFAS Funding?

You’re asking if there’s an age limit for NSFAS funding. No, there isn’t. Regardless of your age, you’re eligible to apply as long as you’re a South African citizen and meet the financial and academic requirements.

What Are the Implications if I Drop Out of My Course While Receiving NSFAS Funding?

If you drop out while receiving NSFAS funding, you’ll likely need to repay the funds. It’s also possible that you may not be eligible for further funding in the future.

Conclusion

In conclusion, understanding the NSFAS application process, checking your status, and knowing the difference between the SASSA R350 Grant and NSFAS is crucial. Always stay informed about your eligibility, necessary documents, and the methods of submitting your application. Remember, there are different ways to receive your NSFAS payment. keep track of your NSFAS status.
