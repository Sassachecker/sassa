Steps: How to Reinstate My Cancelled SRD SASSA Application?

If your application for the SASSA R350 grant has been declined or if your payment was stopped then you can appeal the decision to reinstate your canceled Sassa application. The appeal process involves the following steps:

  1. The first step is that you will need to visit the SASSA official website.
  2. Then you need to add your South African ID number and the cell phone number you are using to apply for funds.
  3. Then click on the button named “send pin”.
  4. Then you will review a pin and you need to enter the received pin that is provided to you if you enter the right link you will be redirected to the reinstate page.
  5. Choose the reason for the rejection of your Sassa application or why you are reinstating the canceled SRD R350 Grant Application.
  6. Then follow the on-screen commands and choose it will ask you that are you sure about the reinstatement grant then choose the “yes” option.
  7. Once you have submitted the appeal then SASSA will consider your appeal and make a decision. You will be notified in writing of the outcome of your appeal.
  8. If your appeal is successful, you will receive the payment. If your appeal is not approved, then you may need to take some further steps, such as making a complaint or seeking legal advice.

SASSA Reconsideration Application 

SASSA reconsideration application is a process that allows citizens of South Africa to request to review the decision made by SASSA regarding their social grant application. If an applicant is not satisfied with a decision made by SASSA, they can request reconsideration of the decision.

The reconsideration application can be submitted in cases where the application is declined or the amount of the grant awarded is lower than it was fixed. Then to request reconsideration, the applicant needs to submit a reinstated application on ar SASSA official website. 

In the reconsideration application, the applicant should provide reasons why they believe the decision made by SASSA is not fair. The applicant should also provide any additional information or documentation that supports their case.

People can do a SASSA reapplication in the following cases:

Their Previous Application was Declined

If an applicant’s previous application for a social grant was declined, they may be able to reapply for the same grant. In this case, the applicant should review the reasons for the initial decline and address any issues before submitting a new application.

Their Circumstances Have Changed

If an applicant’s circumstances have changed since their previous application, they may be eligible for a social grant. For example, if an applicant was previously employed but has since lost their job and is now in need of assistance, they may be eligible for a social grant.

They Missed the Application Deadline

If an applicant missed the application deadline for a social grant, they may be able to reapply during the next application period. It’s important to note that reapplication may not guarantee approval, and applicants should make sure that they meet all the eligibility criteria before reapplying.

Their Grant Has Expired

What is SRD R350 Grant Fund?

The SASSA SRD R350 Grant Fund was a temporary financial assistance program launched by the South African government in response to the economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic. The program provided a monthly cash payment of R350 to eligible South Africans who were unemployed or not receiving any other form of social grant or financial assistance.

The grant was available for six months, but now it has been extended over time, and it aimed to provide temporary relief to individuals who had lost their income or were struggling financially due to the pandemic.

What are the Requirements for the SRD R350 grant?

The requirements for the SRD R350 grant are as follows:

  1. Applicants must be South African citizens or permanent residents with a valid South African ID or asylum seeker permit.
  2. Applicants must be 18 years of age or older.
  3. Applicants must be unemployed and not receive any other social grant or financial assistance.
  4. Applicants must have an income below a certain threshold, 
  5. Applicants must have a bank account, as the grant is paid through direct deposit into the applicant’s bank account.
  6. Applicants must have a mobile phone and valid contact details, as all communication and updates regarding the grant are done through SMS or email.
  7. Applicants must have a valid South African ID or asylum seeker permit.
