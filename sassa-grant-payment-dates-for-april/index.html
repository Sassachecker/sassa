Sassa Grant Payment Dates For April 2023 – Excellent Guide

  • Post published:March 22, 2023

Sassa Grant Payment Dates for April 2023 are announced. The SASSA is responsible for the administration of social grants in South Africa. Social grants are the government’s non-contributory welfare benefits to assist individuals and families who require financial support. 

SASSA grants aim to alleviate poverty and support vulnerable groups such as the elderly, disabled, and children. Several SASSA grants include the Child Support Grant, Old Age Grant, Disability Grant, Foster Child Grant, and Care Dependency Grant. 

The amount of each grant is determined by different factors such as the applicant’s age, income, and level of disability. SASSA grants are paid monthly and can be collected at designated pay points or through various electronic payment methods such as bank transfers and ATMs. 

The grant funds are intended to cover basic needs such as clothing, food, and shelter and are not meant to replace regular income. The administration of SASSA grants has been criticized due to payment delays and fraudulent activities. However, Saasa has tried to address these challenges and improve the delivery of social grants to those in need.

Sassa Grant Payment Dates for April 2023 (Social Grant)

Here are the SASSA grant payment dates for the payment for April 2023:

  • The payout date for the Elderly Person Grant fund is April 2, 2023.
  • The payment date for the Disabled Persons Grant is April 3, 2023.
  • However, the payment for foster care will be released by April 8, 2023. 
  • The child support and care dependency grants will also be on April 8, 2023. 
  • The payment date for funds is April 6, 2023.

Eligibility criteria for Sassa grant fund

SASSA provides social grants to assist vulnerable individuals and households in South Africa. The eligibility criteria for SASSA grant funds depend on the specific grant that an individual is applying for. Here are the general eligibility criteria for SASSA grant funds:

  • The applicant must be a South African citizen or permanent resident and must be over the age of 18 years.
  • The applicant must be unemployed or, if employed, earn less than the set maximum amount per month, which varies depending on the grant type.
  • SASSA conducts a means test to determine if the applicant’s income and assets are below a certain threshold.
  • The applicant must reside in South Africa and have a valid ID or permit. For disability grants, the applicant must be medically certified as having a permanent disability affecting their workability.
  • The applicant must be medically certified for care dependency grants requiring full-time care.
  • For child support grants, the applicant must have children under 18 years who live with them.

FAQs About Sassa Grant Payment Dates For April

SASSA offers a range of grants to eligible individuals, including the old age grant, disability grants, child support grants, foster child grants, and grant-in-aid. Each grant’s funding varies depending on various factors, such as the individual’s age and needs.

To apply for a SASSA grant, you must fill out an application form and provide a range of documentation to support your application. This may include your ID book, proof of income, medical reports, and other relevant documents. You can apply for a SASSA grant online, in person at a SASSA office, or via a designated agent in your area.

SASSA grants are paid monthly by directly depositing your bank account or cash at a designated pay point. You can choose how you want to receive your funds, but it is recommended that you use the direct deposit option for safety and convenience.

SASSA grant funds can be used to pay for necessities such as food, clothing, and shelter. There are no restrictions on how you use the funds, but it is recommended that you use them responsibly and avoid spending them on non-essential items.
