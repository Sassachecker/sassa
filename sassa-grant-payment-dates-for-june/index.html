The South African Social Security Agency (Sassa) is vital in providing financial assistance to vulnerable individuals and families nationwide. Through various grant programs, Sassa aims to alleviate poverty, support disabled individuals, and ensure the well-being of children and older people.

One crucial aspect of these grant programs is knowing the payment dates, enabling beneficiaries to plan their finances and meet their essential needs. This article will delve into the Sassa Grant payment dates for June.

We will explore the dates on which different grant categories, such as the Old Age Pension, Disability Grant, Child Support Grant, Foster Child Grant, and Care Dependency Grant, are expected to be disbursed. 

By understanding these payment dates, beneficiaries can effectively manage their budgets and make informed financial decisions. So let’s now dive into the details of June’s Sassa Grant payment dates, ensuring that individuals and families relying on these grants are well-informed and prepared.

  • The Elderly Person Grant will be paid out on June 2, 2023,
  • The Disabled Persons Grant will be disbursed on June 3, 2023.

Types of SASSA grant funds

The South African Social Security Agency (Sassa) provides various grant funds to needy individuals and families. These grants address specific circumstances and challenge vulnerable individuals face nationwide. Here are some of the key types of Sassa grant funds:

Old Age Pension

This grant aims to provide financial support to elderly South Africans who have reached the qualifying age (60 years and older for men and 55 years and older for women). The Old Age Pension Grant helps seniors meet their basic needs and improve their quality of life.

Disability Grant

This grant assists individuals with disabilities who cannot financially support themselves due to their physical, mental, or sensory impairments. It provides financial aid to help with daily living expenses, medical needs, and other requirements related to their disability.

Child Support Grant

This grant is targeted at caregivers or parents of children who are below the age of 18 and in need of financial assistance. The Child Support Grant aims to improve the well-being and development of children by providing funds for their basic needs, including food, clothing, and education.

Foster Child Grant

This grant is provided to foster parents or caregivers who have taken on the responsibility of caring for children who are not biologically related to them. It assists with the additional costs of providing foster children with a nurturing and stable environment, such as food, education, healthcare, and other essential needs.

Care Dependency Grant

This grant supports caregivers responsible for caring for and supporting children or adults with severe disabilities or medical conditions. The Care Dependency Grant helps cover the costs of specialized care, medical treatments, assistive devices, and other necessary support services.

Grant-in-Aid

This grant offers financial assistance to individuals dependent on others due to a disability or chronic illness. In addition, it assists with additional costs related to personal care, transportation, and other support services required for daily living.

Eligibility criteria for Sassa grant fund

The eligibility criteria for Sassa grant funds in South Africa vary depending on the specific grant category. However, here are the general guidelines for eligibility:

1.       Old Age Pension:

  • Must be a South African citizen or permanent resident.
  • Must be 60 years or older for men and 55 years or older for women.
  • Must meet the means test, which assesses income and assets.

2.       Disability Grant:

  • Must be a South African citizen or permanent resident.
  • Must have a physical, mental, or sensory disability that prevents employment.
  • Must meet the means test.

3.       Child Support Grant:

  • Must be a South African citizen or permanent resident.
  • Must be the primary caregiver of a child under the age of 18.
  • Must meet the means test (income threshold).

4.       Foster Child Grant:

  • Must be a South African citizen or permanent resident.
  • Must be the registered foster parent or caregiver of a foster child.
  • Must meet the means test (income threshold).

5.       Care Dependency Grant:

  • Must be a South African citizen or permanent resident.
  • Must be the primary caregiver of a child or adult with a severe disability or medical condition.
  • Must provide medical evidence of the disability or condition.
  • Must meet the means test (income threshold).

6.       Grant-in-Aid:

  • Must be a South African citizen or permanent resident.
  • Must be dependent on another person due to a disability or chronic illness.
  • Must meet the means test (income threshold).
  • It’s important to note that these eligibility criteria are general guidelines, and specific requirements may vary. Additionally, the means test assesses the applicant’s income, assets, and overall financial situation to determine eligibility.
