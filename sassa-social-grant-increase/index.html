SASSA Social Grant Increase for 2023 2024 [Said Ramaphosa]

  • Post published:March 7, 2023

The Sassa social grant increased from 2023 to 2024 because many South Africans face ongoing economic challenges. While the grant has provided some relief to those most in need, critics have argued that it is not enough to address the underlying causes of poverty in the country.

The R350 SRD grant was initially introduced in May 2020 to provide temporary financial relief to unemployed and vulnerable individuals affected by the pandemic and the related lockdown measures. The grant was intended to provide a short-term solution to the immediate financial needs of those most severely impacted by the crisis.

Sassa R350 Grant Extended by Another Year 2024

The decision to extend the R350 SRD grant for another year in 2024 is extended and welcomed by many South Africans still struggling to make ends meet due to the economic impact. 

The decision to extend the R350 SRD grant reflects the ongoing need for social support and financial relief in the face of persistent poverty and inequality in South Africa. 

This extension will help ensure South African citizens can access necessities such as food and shelter. The grant is a crucial lifeline for many vulnerable South Africans, and its continuation is a welcome development in the ongoing fight against poverty.

SASSA R350 SRD Grant will Continue Due To Poverty

The SASSA initiated the R350 SRD grant in 2020 to provide emergency financial assistance to unemployed and vulnerable South Africans affected by the COVID-19 pandemic. The grant was initially set to run from May 2020 to October 2020 but has been extended several times.

However, due to the ongoing impact of the pandemic on the economy and the livelihoods of many South Africans, there have been many demands to continue the grant due to poverty and unemployment because it is a major challenge in South Africa, and many people are struggling to manage their finance.

Moreover, in response to the people’s demands and needs, the government announced that the SRD grant would be extended for 2024. The extension of the grant is welcomed by the citizens of South Africa, as this is much-needed relief to those struggling to survive.

The continuing of the SRD grant is an important step towards poverty in South Africa. While it may not be a long-term solution, it temporarily relieves citizens struggling to fulfill their finances. 

Will the SRD Grant Extension Mean Payments be Increased?

The SRD grant is a social assistance program the South African government provides to support unemployed citizens in dire need of financial assistance. The government has extended the SRD grant recently for 2023 and 2024.

However, these extensions do not necessarily mean the payment amount will increase because Sassa has clarified that the SRD grant remains R350 per month. 

The rise in inflation and unemployment has become a big challenge for the people of South Africa. However, to overcome inflation and unemployment, a report reveals that Sassa is looking to make a mechanism to give permanent grants to their citizens.

What does Ramaphosa Say About the Sassa Grants Increase?

Recently in the state of Nations Address (SONA), President Cyril Ramaphosa announced, “We will ensure that existing social grants are increased to cushion the poor against rising inflation.” This means that the SASSA grants will be increased by next year.

Ramaphosa further said that the SRD grant has reached 7.8 million South African citizens of South Africa and helping them manage their finances. However, With the announcement of an increase in the payment amount for Sassa, it is important to note that the details of the increase, including the amount and timing, have yet to be announced.

SRD Grant Will Continue: Says Ramaphosa

South African President Cyril Ramaphosa has announced that the government will continue the Social Relief of Distress (SRD) grant program until 2024. The SRD grant provides a monthly payment of R350 to eligible South Africans who are unemployed and do not receive any other income support.

The SRD grant program must be extended due to the ongoing economic impact, which has left many South Africans in financial distress. The announcement of the continuation of the SRD grant program has been welcomed by many who rely on the monthly payment to cover basic expenses.

Increase in Sassa social Grant fund 2023

Social grants are financial assistance that the South African government provides to support their citizens who cannot support themselves due to poverty, disability, or old age. These social grants are paid monthly and designed to provide financial relief to fulfill necessities such as food, clothing, and shelter.

The government reviews the value of social grants annually, considering factors such as inflation, unemployment, and the cost of living. The government decided to increase the value of social grants to ensure they remain in line with the rising cost of living.

The South African government announced increased social grant funds, including grants provided by the South African Social Security Agency (SASSA). The increase was effective from 1 April 2023 and was meant to help alleviate the financial burden many South Africans faced due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The social grant funds increment included an increase of:

  • The pension for old age was R1895 now. It is increased to R2085 per month.
  • The grant for disabled people has been increased from R1900 to R2085 monthly. 
  • People older than 75 will have an Old Age grant increase from R2,005 to R2,105.
  • War Veterans grant increased from R2,005 to R2,105
  • The grant for child support is increased from R480 to R505.
  • The foster care grant has been increased from R1070 to R1,125 per month. 
  • The grant for the care is also increased from R1985 to R2085 per month.

Steps to apply for the Sassa Grant fund

If you are a permanent citizen of South Africa  and looking for the procedure  to apply for one of the SASSA grant funds to get the financial support to fulfill your basic needs, you can follow these steps:

  1. The first step to applying for a Sassa grant is determining your eligibility.
  2. To check the eligibility of different grants, Visit the SASSA website to determine for which grant you are eligible based on the criteria age, disability status, and income.
  3. Next, get a hold of all your necessary documents before applying.
  4. Then you can download the application from the SAASA website or get it from your nearest SASSA office.
  5. Next, fill out the application form completely without any incorrect information.
  6. Then submit the completed application form and all the necessary documents by sending it through the post or to your nearest SASSA office.  You can also submit your application online through the SASSA website.
  7. Lastly, SASSA will review the application and assess it to check if you are eligible and then inform you of the outcome.

FAQs About SASSA Social Grant Increases

The SASSA Social Grant Extension is an extension of the social grants program provided by the South African government. The extension aims to provide additional financial support to eligible South African citizens struggling financially due to the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic.

All social grants SASSA provides, including old age grants, disability grants, child support grants, foster child grants, and care dependency, are eligible for the extension.

The SASSA Social Grant Extension was introduced in May 2020 and extended several times. The current extension is set to end in 2024.

Existing social grant beneficiaries eligible for the extension do not need to apply separately for additional financial support. Instead, the extension will be automatically applied to their monthly payments.
