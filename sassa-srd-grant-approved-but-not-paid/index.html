SASSA SRD Grant Approved But Not Paid – [Solution]

  • Post published:March 5, 2023

If you have applied for the SASSA SRD grant and your status says “Approved” but have not received any payment, you are not alone. Many people have been experiencing the same issue, which can be frustrating and confusing. In this article, we will explore why your R350 grant says approved, but no payment has been made, what to do if you have not received your SASSA grant payment, and the reasons behind the delayed R350 grant payments.

Background of SASSA SRD Grant Approved But Not Paid

In May 2020, SASSA introduced the SRD grant in response to the COVID-19 pandemic. The grant is meant to offer immediate financial help to those unable to care for their families or themselves due to COVID-19-related issues.

Applicants must be unemployed, not getting any other social assistance, and have no source of income to be eligible for the SRD grant. The grant is meant to provide help for six months and is paid R350 monthly.

Although many people have benefited from the SRD grant, there have been numerous reports of people who were approved for the grant but never got any money.

SASSA SRD Grant Approved but not Paid

There could be several reasons if your SASSA SRD grant status is approved, but no payment has been made. It is crucial to check the few things that can contribute to delays in the R350 award payments. The SASSA is trying to ensure eligible recipients get their payments despite the delays.

Why Does My R350 Say Approved but No Payment?

Your R350 grant status may say “Approved” for several reasons, but you have not received any payment. Some of the most prevalent reasons include the following.

Verification Process

Once your application has been received, it goes through a verification process to ensure you meet the SASSA grant eligibility criteria. This process can take some time, and your application may have been approved, but the payment has not yet been processed.

Incorrect Banking Details

If you have provided incorrect banking details, the payment may have been rejected by the bank, and you can face the issue of Sassa r350 being approved but with no payment date. In this case, you must update your banking details with SASSA and wait for the payment to be processed again.

System Errors

System errors or technical glitches may prevent the payment from being processed, and you can face the issue Sassa r350 grant being approved but with no pay date. If this is the case, you may have to contact SASSA to report the issue and determine when the payment will be made.

Fraudulent Activity

Fraudulent activity may sometimes be detected during the verification process, and the payment may be delayed or canceled. If this is the case, you will need to contact SASSA to determine steps to rectify the situation.

Backlog

SASSA is experiencing a backlog due to many SRD grant submissions. Applications and payments have been delayed due to this backlog, frustrating applicants.

Have You Not Received Your SASSA Grant Payment?

If you have not received your SASSA grant payment, there are some things you can do to check the status of your application.

Check Your Status Online

You can check the status of your SASSA grant application online by visiting the SASSA website and entering your ID number and other relevant details.

Contact SASSA

If you cannot check your status online or need more information, you can contact SASSA directly by calling their toll-free number or visiting your nearest SASSA office.

Check Your Banking Details

If you have not received your payment, you may have provided incorrect banking details. Make sure to check that your banking details are correct and up-to-date.

Be Patient

Due to the backlog in processing applications and payments, your payment might take some time to complete. Therefore, it is crucial to show patience while you wait for your payment and to keep confirming with SASSA that it is being processed.

Seek Help

If you have financial issues and your SRD grant has not been paid, asking for help from other groups or governmental agencies might be beneficial. While you wait for your SRD grant payment, some organizations, like nonprofits and community groups, might be able to give you temporary help. In addition, if you qualify, the Department of Social Development might be able to offer you other types of social assistance.

Complain to SASSA

If you have tried everything else and your SRD grant payment is still delayed, you can complain to SASSA. SASSA has set up a complaints resolution procedure to assist people with problems receiving grant payments.

Administrative Issues with the SRD Grant

Although the SRD grant was created to offer desperately needed assistance to poor people and families during the COVID-19 pandemic, the program has had several administrative difficulties. Many applicants have been frustrated and anxious due to these difficulties, which have led to delays and uncertainty regarding the payment of approved grants.

The SRD grant’s verification procedure presents one of the most significant administrative difficulties. Individuals must submit various documents, such as identification, residency, and salary proofs, to be considered for the grant. Before approving the grant, SASSA must validate this information, which can take some time. Verification delays occasionally cause payment delays or even the rejection of an approved applicant.

Why Were Many of the R350 Grant Payments Delayed?

The R350 grant payments were delayed due to several reasons, including.

Verification Process

As mentioned earlier, the verification process can take some time, which was one of the main reasons behind the delayed payments.

Technical Issues

Technical issues and system errors also caused delays in payment processing.

High Volume of Applications

The high volume of applications also strains the system, leading to delays in processing payments.

Fraudulent Activity

SASSA also detected fraudulent activity, which led to delays and cancellations of some payments.
