[Updated] Sassa SRD Grants For Refugees and Asylum Seekers

  • Post published:June 21, 2023

In South Africa, a support system known as the SASSA SRD Grants extends a helping hand to refugees and asylum seekers. Sassa SRD grants for refugees serve as a lifeline, providing financial assistance and a sense of security to those displaced from their homelands due to conflict or persecution.

For many refugees and asylum seekers, the journey to safety is fraught with challenges, and their new lives often start from scratch. Recognizing this, the South African Social Security Agency (SASSA) has implemented these grants to alleviate some of the burdens this vulnerable group faces.

Not only do these grants offer a vital financial safety net, but they also contribute to the social integration and overall well-being of refugees and asylum seekers. By easing the financial strain, the grants enable individuals and families to access basic needs, such as food, shelter, education, and healthcare.

Eligibility criteria of SASSA SRD Grants for Refugees and Asylum seekers

Applicants had to fulfill specific requirements to be eligible for the SRD R350 grant. These criteria ensured the grants were allocated to individuals who genuinely needed financial assistance. The eligibility criteria were as follows:

  1. Applicants were required to be residing within South Africa.
  2.  South African Citizen: Individuals who hold South African citizenship.
  3. Individuals who have obtained refugee status in South Africa.
  4. Individuals who have applied for asylum are awaiting a decision.
  5. Individuals who hold a special permit in South Africa.
  6. Applicants must fall between the age range of 18 and 60 years.
  7. Applicants must meet the means test, which involves having a monthly income of R624 or less.
  8. Applicants must be unemployed and not receive financial support from any other source.
  9. Applicants must demonstrate insufficient financial means to support themselves adequately.
  10. Social Grants: Applicants must not be receiving any other social grants.
  11. Applicants must not be contributing to or eligible for UIF payment.
  12. Applicants must not receive a National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) stipend.

How to apply for SRD Grant as an Asylum Seekers or Special Permit Holder?

To apply for the SRD grant as an asylum seeker or special permit holder, follow these steps:

  1. As an asylum seeker in South Africa or a special permit holder from countries such as Zimbabwe, Angola, or Lesotho, ensure that you meet the specific criteria for the SRD grant.
  2. Access the official website of the South African Social Security Agency (SASSA) and navigate to the SRD grant application page.
  3. On the application page, look for the section that addresses asylum seekers and special permit holders. Click on the relevant link to access the correct application form.
  4. Fill out the application form with your details, including your names, contact information, and relevant identification numbers, such as your asylum seeker file or special permit number. Make sure to enter the most up-to-date information.
  5. Attach the necessary supporting documents to your application. These may include a copy of your valid asylum seeker permit or special permit, proof of identity, proof of residence, and any other documents requested by SASSA.
  6. Once you have completed the form and attached the required documents, submit your application online through the designated platform on the SASSA website. Make sure to double-check all the information provided before submitting.
  7. Stay informed about its progress after submitting it. SASSA may communicate with you regarding the status of your application, so regularly check your email or the online portal for updates.

Remember, if your asylum seeker or special permit has expired, you must re-apply for the SRD grant using the new permit or file number. Payments for the approved months, when your permit was still valid, will still be processed. Stay proactive and ensure your documentation is current to receive the support you are entitled to.

What Documents must I submit when I apply for the R350 grant?

When applying for the R350 grant, SASSA does not require specific documents to be submitted during the application process. Unlike other grant types, the SRD grant application does not require applicants to provide supporting documentation. The application process is conducted electronically, ensuring a streamlined and accessible application experience.

To apply for the R350 grant, applicants only need a cellphone number to initiate the application. However, it is crucial to have continuous access to the provided cellphone number as SASSA will use it to communicate with applicants and provide feedback on their application status.

This simplified approach removes barriers and makes the application process more accessible to individuals needing financial support. In addition, by eliminating the need for physical documentation, applicants can submit their information electronically, saving time and effort.

However, it is important to note that while documentation is not required during the initial application, SASSA may request additional information or documentation later for verification. Therefore, ensuring that accurate and truthful information is provided during the application process is essential.

How can I check if my SRD application was successful?

To check the status of your SRD application and determine if it was successful, you can use the online portal provided by SASSA. First, access the designated page to check your SRD grant status by following the link provided. 

Enter the required information, such as your identification details or application reference number, to retrieve your application status. In addition, if your SRD grant application was declined, you must know you can appeal this decision. 

How will my SRD Grant be paid?

Once your SRD grant application has been approved and a confirmed pay date has been provided after checking your status, you can collect your grant payment from designated collection points. 

These collection points include Pick ‘n Pay or Boxer stores, where you can receive your grant payment. To collect your SRD grant payment, you will need to have the following information with you:

  1. Payment ID: This is a system-generated number provided by SASSA. Having your Payment ID on hand is essential during the collection process.
  2. Cellphone number: The cellphone number you used during the application process is required for verification when collecting your grant payment.

If you are unsure about your Payment ID, you can easily look it up by visiting the provided link. You must provide your asylum seeker File Number, special permit holder Passport Number, and the cellphone number used during the application.

In case you need to update or change your cellphone number, it is recommended to contact the SASSA call center at 0800 60 10 11. They will assist you with the necessary steps to ensure your contact information is updated accurately.

FAQs About SASSA SRD Grants For Refugees and Asylum Seekers

To be eligible for SASSA SRD Grants as a refugee or asylum seeker, you must have a valid refugee status or be an asylum seeker in South Africa. Other eligibility factors include:

  • Meeting the means test.
  • Being unemployed.
  • Having insufficient financial means to support yourself.
  • Not receiving any other social grants.

If your SASSA SRD Grant application is declined, you can appeal with the Department of Social Development. You can find information on submitting an appeal for your SRD Grant on the relevant platforms. It is important to submit an appeal for each month your grant was declined.

SASSA SRD Grant payments for refugees and asylum seekers can be collected at designated collection points, such as Pick ‘n Pay or Boxer stores. You will need your Payment ID (system-generated number) and the cellphone number used during the application process to collect your grant payment.

Conclusion of SASSA SRD Grants For Refugees and Asylum Seekers

SASSA SRD Grants support refugees and asylum seekers in South Africa who are facing economic hardships. These grants serve as a lifeline for individuals who may not have access to other forms of assistance. 

By meeting the specific eligibility criteria, refugees and asylum seekers can apply for various SRD grants tailored to their needs, such as child support, disability, and older person grants.
