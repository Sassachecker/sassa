Types of SASSA Grant Funds: Excellent Guide (You Must Read)

  • Post published:June 5, 2023

SASSA is a South African government institute responsible for providing social grants to the needy citizens of South Africa. SASSA grants financial support to eligible individuals, such as the elderly, disabled, and orphans. The types of SASSA grants include the old age pension, care dependency grant, disability grant, and foster care grant.

A specific process for SASSA grants involves verifying the grant recipient’s eligibility and paying into their designated bank account or onto a SASSA-issued grant card. Moreover, recipients must reapply for the grant annually and provide updated information, such as their banking details, to ensure that payments are made correctly.

Types of Sassa Grant Funds for Citizens

The SASSA provides various types of grants to eligible individuals who need financial support. The following are the main types of SASSA grants:

Old Age Person Grant

The SASSA provides an Old Age Pension grant to eligible South Africans over 60 who do not have a source of income. This grant will provide a safety net for older people who require financial support.

However, it is important that individuals need to be eligible for the old age grant. The individual must be a South African citizen or a permanent resident over the age of 60 and do not have a regular source of income. However, it is important to provide proof of their age, residency status, and income status.

Disabled Person Grant

The SASSA Disability Grant is given to individuals who are permanently disabled and unable to earn a living due to their disability. This grant is meant to provide financial support to those in need and improve their standard of living.

To the eligibility of the Disability Grant, individuals must be South African citizens or permanent residents who cannot earn a living due to their disability. They must also provide proof of their disability and residency status. SASSA is committed to providing accurate and timely grant payments to eligible recipients and improving the standard of living for disabled South Africans.

Child Support Grant 

For people who are in need of child support grants, SASSA provides them a support grant to the primary caregiver of a child under 18. This grant is designed to provide financial support to help with the costs of raising a child.

To get the grant, the primary caregiver must be eligible for the Child Support Grant. You need to be a South African citizen or permanent resident, and provide proof of residency status and the child’s birth certificate. The child must also be under the age of 18 and must be living with the primary caregiver.

The grant is paid either into the recipient’s designated bank account or onto a SASSA-issued grant card. Recipients must reapply for the grant annually and provide updated information to ensure that payments are made correctly.

Foster Care Grant fund 

The SASSA provides a Foster Care Grant to individuals caring for a foster child and unable to work due to their care responsibilities. This grant is designed to provide financial support to help with the costs of raising a foster child.

To be eligible for the Foster Care Grant, the caregiver must be a South African citizen or permanent resident, provide proof of residency status and the foster child’s birth certificate, and care for the foster child full-time. The foster child must also be under the age of 18 and must be living with the caregiver.

If the person is eligible, SASSA provides accurate and timely grant payments to recipients and improves foster children’s living standards.

Care dependency Grant

The SASSA provides a Care Dependency Grant to individuals who are caring for a person with a severe disability and are unable to work due to their care responsibilities. This grant is designed to provide financial support to help with the costs of caring for a dependent person.

To be eligible for the Care Dependency Grant, the caregiver must be a South African citizen or permanent resident, must provide proof of their residency status and the dependent person’s disability, and must be caring for the dependent person full-time. In addition, the dependent person must also be severely disabled and unable to care for themselves.

SASSA Grant-In-Aid

The Grant in-aid is given to the caretaker of those people who could not work to earn for themselves. These caretakers completely look after these people from their feed to health, from their basic nesscities to others. 

To apply for the SASSA grant in-aid application, you need to fulfill some requirements, you must be a resident and citizen of South Africa. You must need to submit some documents like articles of association, byelaws, audited statements of accounts, sources and patterns of income and expenditure, etc. 

SASSA SRD R350 

This is the special grant introduced after the pandemic of COVID-19, called the Social Relief of Distress. This grant is especially for those needy people who lost their decent living hood during the times of pandemic. 

This grant may help those people to build themselves again, and it will help them to start their businesses or to give almost the same lives to their families as they were living before the pandemic. 

War Veterans Grant

The South African government took charge of granting all the soldiers and their families who lost a lot of them during and after World War II or in the Korean War. 

The requirements are almost similar to other grants, as you have to be a citizen and resident of South Africa. You must have your ID documents, marriage certificate, and optional spouse documents. If you have you must submit them. 

How to Apply for the SASSA Grant Funds?

In the majority of South Africa’s main cities and towns, SASSA has offices. Visit an SASSA location close to you to submit an application for a grant. You can also visit their official website to apply online for the grant.

You must have to submit some compulsory documents, such as proof of your identification, residency status, and disability, when you visit an SASSA location (if applicable). Additionally, you must submit the information about your earnings, assets, and the individual you care for (if applicable).

Frequently Asked Questions

You must be the citizen and resident of South Africa between the age of 18 to 60. Also you must have you ID documents along with the proof of your unemployment. 

The SASSA monthly grants are from R1980 to R2100, depending on the category. 

SASSA releases the payments multiple times in a month, but once for one category. 

All the oldies, foster and child care, disabled, care dependencies, war veteran who are eligible on the criteria will be given the SASSA grant each month. 

Conclusion

To qualified South African citizens and permanent residents who fulfill the requirements, SASSA offers various types of SASSA grants. old age grant, disability grant, child support rant, foster child grant, care dependency grant, war veterans grant, and grant in-aid are the categories. Depending on the type of grant, there are differences in each grant’s amount and eligibility requirements.
